Background

What is Skull Stripping?

Skull stripping, in the context of medical imaging and neuroimaging, is a process used to remove or separate the skull and other non-brain tissues from an image or scan of the brain. The primary goal of skull stripping is to isolate the brain tissue so that it can be analyzed, measured, or reconstructed accurately without interference from surrounding structures such as the skull, scalp, or soft tissues. This is a crucial step in various neuroimaging applications, including brain segmentation, volume measurement, and the creation of 3D brain models for research and medical diagnosis. Skull stripping algorithms and techniques are commonly used in MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging) and CT (Computed Tomography) scans of the brain to improve the accuracy of brain analysis.
